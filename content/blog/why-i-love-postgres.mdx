title: "Why I Reach for Postgres First"
date: "2026-05-02"
summary: "It's not just a database — it's a queue, a cache, a search engine, and a JSON store when you need it."
---

People ask why I default to PostgreSQL for almost everything. The honest answer:
it does 90% of what teams reach for extra infrastructure to do — with one
operational surface to understand.

## One database, many jobs

- **Queues:** `SELECT ... FOR UPDATE SKIP LOCKED` gives you a solid job queue.
- **JSON:** `jsonb` columns handle semi-structured data without a second store.
- **Search:** full-text search covers most product needs before Elasticsearch.
- **Analytics:** window functions and CTEs replace a lot of app-side code.

## Boring is a feature

Postgres has been battle-tested for decades. The failure modes are documented,
the tooling is mature, and the community answers are usually correct.

When you genuinely outgrow it, you'll know — and you'll have real numbers to
justify the next piece of infrastructure.
